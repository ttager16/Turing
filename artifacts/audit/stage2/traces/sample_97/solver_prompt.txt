### Problem Statement

In a **global collaborative text editing platform**, thousands of users simultaneously modify millions of distributed documents.  
Each document is divided into multiple text **segments**, allowing independent editing, synchronization, and offline operation.  
The challenge is to maintain **consistency, concurrency control, and efficient prefix-based querying** under extreme scale — ensuring all edits remain conflict-free and recoverable after disconnections.

To achieve this, you must implement a **hybrid data structure** that combines:
- A **Trie** for prefix-based text searches, and  
- A **Segment-based Index** for partitioned text management and offline merge simulation.

This system should handle **real-time insertions, deletions, searches, and merges**, preserving all user changes while ensuring that concurrent edits do not corrupt shared data.

---

### Objectives

1. **Hybrid Structure Integration**  
   Combine a Trie with a segment-based indexing approach to support fast lookups and dynamic segment management.

2. **Conflict Resolution**  
   When multiple users edit the same segment, ensure all versions are preserved and merged deterministically.

3. **Offline Merge Simulation**  
   Support operations that merge offline edits safely into the main shared state.

4. **Real-Time Search**  
   Allow prefix-based and segment-based searches that retrieve all relevant text fragments efficiently.

5. **Fault Tolerance and Efficiency**  
   Handle several hundred sequential operations while maintaining internal consistency and preventing corruption.
