### Problem Statement
In a sprawling national healthcare network, the government must distribute specialized medical agencies across a large set of interconnected regions. Each region has unique population densities, complex healthcare demands, and varying operational costs that fluctuate based on how agencies are shared between neighboring regions. This problem requires sophisticated multi-level coordination and coverage verification with strict budget constraints, complex adjacency rules, regional priority systems, and temporal dependencies.

### Cost and Coverage Rules
1. **Base Cost Calculation**: The cost of placing an agency in region i is calculated as: `base_cost[i] = (population[i] / 1000) + (demands[i] * 1.5) + (i * 0.5)` where the third term represents infrastructure setup cost that increases with region index.

2. **Agency Coverage**: Each agency covers exactly one region. Adjacent regions (indices i and i+1) can share an agency, which covers both regions with a single placement. When shared, the agency must be placed at the region with higher demand. If demands are equal, place at the region with higher population. If both are equal, place at the lower index.

3. **Shared Agency Cost with Dynamic Discount**: When an agency covers two adjacent regions:
   - If both regions have population < 3000: discount = 0.80 (20% savings)
   - If one region has population >= 3000 and < 6000: discount = 0.85 (15% savings)
   - If one region has population >= 6000: discount = 0.90 (10% savings)
   - Cost = `discount * base_cost[higher_demand_region]`

4. **Priority Region Constraint**: Regions where `(demands[i] > 40 OR population[i] > 8000)` are designated as "priority regions" and have additional constraints:
   - A priority region can ONLY be covered individually OR share with exactly one adjacent non-priority region
   - Two adjacent priority regions CANNOT share an agency
   - If a priority region shares with a non-priority region, there's NO discount (multiplier = 1.0)

5. **Chain Constraint**: If region i is covered by a shared agency with region i+1, then region i+2 (if exists) CANNOT be part of a shared agency with region i+1. This prevents chain formations: `[i-i+1 shared] -> [i+1-i+2 shared]` is INVALID.

6. **Cluster Penalty**: If three or more consecutive agencies are all individual (non-shared), add a cluster penalty of 5.0 to the total cost for each such sequence of 3+ individual agencies.

7. **Budget Constraint**: The max_cost is the total budget limit. The sum of all agency placement costs (including cluster penalties) must not exceed max_cost.

8. **Minimum Coverage Requirement**: Regions with demands >= 35 must receive coverage from an agency where the placement region's demand is also >= 30 (quality assurance constraint).

9. **Objective**: Minimize the number of agencies while ensuring every region is covered, total cost ≤ max_cost, and all constraints (priority, chain, cluster, minimum coverage) are satisfied.
