Problem Statement:
### Problem Statement

TransLogistics Inc. has expanded to handle global-scale deliveries across an intricate, dynamic network of interconnected regions, each with its own operational nuances. The company must orchestrate a fleet of specialized vehicles each with custom load capacities, fuel consumption patterns, and route preferences through a multi-layered system of delivery hubs and transit corridors. These hubs are organized into graph-like structures that may shift topologically due to unpredictable factors such as closed roads, fluctuating traffic patterns, and enforced transit regulations.

To achieve a robust scheduling and routing plan, TransLogistics Inc. requires a solution that uses shortest-path search and graph-based dynamic programming for small instances, and a greedy heuristic with a simple flow-like demand allocator for larger cases. It must handle overlapping time windows and real-time route updates. Fuel usage is optimized heuristically while obeying the operational rules below:

1. **Compound Time Windows:** Cities or hubs may have staggered or overlapping delivery windows, requiring detailed scheduling of partial vehicle arrivals.
2. **Fuel Efficiency and Cost Awareness:** Since the fleet contains multiple fuel-consuming vehicle types, the solution must carefully distribute the total load to minimize aggregate fuel cost.

### Objectives

1. Build scheduling over a directed, mutable graph where edges can be disabled or re-weighted via deterministic updates.
2. Provide an optional demand allocator that distributes vehicle capacity to city demands per phase, respecting per-city `hub_capacity`.
3. When `phases` are provided, keep a vehicle’s first chosen route persistent across phases (no reassignment).
4. Produce a schedule listing each vehicle’s path (single-city stay or shortest path), total travel time (edge time only), and fuel cost.

### Constraints

- Vehicle capacity is not enforced along routes; it informs cost and demand allocation only.
- Fuel usage is optimized heuristically; small instances use DP on a route-value objective, larger instances use a greedy strategy.
  - For DP (small instances): maximize route-value = 10 \* number_of_cities_visited - fuel_cost(minutes, capacity). Fuel cost uses the formula in the constraints below.
  - For the greedy heuristic (large instances): process vehicles in ascending `(capacity, id)` order; for each vehicle, assign the highest-value non-overlapping candidate route (same route-value as DP); if none exists, assign an empty route.
- Each vehicle can only be assigned to one route at a time and cannot be split across multiple simultaneous routes.
- Travel times between cities must be non-negative integers representing minutes.
- Vehicle IDs must be unique positive integers, and city names must be non-empty strings.
- The solution must handle cases where no feasible route exists for a vehicle (return empty route with zero costs).
- All routes must start and end at valid cities from the input cities list.
- Fuel costs must be calculated as non-negative integers based on travel time (minutes) and vehicle capacity per the formula below.
- The total number of vehicles assigned to routes cannot exceed the number of available vehicles.
- The solution must be deterministic, given the same input, it should produce the same output.
- Travel graph is directed; 'A-B' does not imply 'B-A'.
- Traffic updates are applied in deterministic order (by route key; removals before additions/changes), and `None` removes the edge.
- Candidate routes are limited to single-city stays and shortest paths between ordered city pairs.
- Without `demands`/`phases`, any city may be visited by at most one vehicle in the final plan; with `demands` or `phases`, multiple vehicles may serve the same city.
- For small instances (<=18 cities and <=12 vehicles) dynamic programming is used; otherwise a greedy heuristic is used.
- Per-vehicle candidate options are truncated to at most 30 after scoring.
- Across phases, a vehicle’s first chosen route persists; later phases do not reassign it.
- TravelTime sums only edge traversal times; waiting to meet time windows is excluded.
- Schedules report arrival times only, in hours rounded to two decimals.
- Fuel cost formula: base_rate=0.1 per minute, efficiency factor (1 + capacity/200), integer truncation, minimum cost 1 for any positive travel time.
- Vehicles are processed in `(capacity, id)` order for tie-breaking.
- Time windows are converted from hours to minutes internally; schedules convert minutes back to hours.
- The global time origin is minute 0; initial arrival is at max(0, start_window).
