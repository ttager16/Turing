### Problem Statement

In a critical phase of Martian colonization, two specialized rovers—a heavy-duty **Builder** and a nimble **Scout**—are deployed to finalize a habitat construction site. The rovers operate on a 5x5 grid of varied elevation and must contend with structurally unsound terrain.

The rovers draw power from a shared, limited supply. Your primary objective is to find the sequence of actions that allows the **Builder** to reach a designated target cell and perform a "Finalize Site" action, all while consuming the **minimum possible total power**.

The terrain contains some **unstable cells**, which are initially impassable. The Scout is equipped with ground-penetrating radar and must first perform a **Scan** action on an adjacent unstable cell to analyze and certify it as stable, making it traversable for both rovers.

### Rover Specifications & Actions

The mission's success depends on the unique capabilities of each rover:

#### **Rover 1: The Builder**

  * **Move:** Can move to any single adjacent square (including diagonals).
      * **Power Cost:** `1 + max(0, h_new - h_old)`
  * **Build:** After moving, it needs to deposit reinforced regolith on a square adjacent to its new position, increasing that square's elevation by one.
      * **Power Cost:** `2` units.
  * **Finalize Site:** A special action that can only be performed when the Builder is at the `target_pos`. This action completes the mission.
      * **Power Cost:** `10` units.

#### **Rover 2: The Scout**

  * **Move:** Can move 1 or 2 squares in a single, straight line (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally). The path must be clear of obstacles (the other rover, unstable cells, or max-height cells).
      * **Power Cost:** `(1 + max(0, h_final - h_initial)) * distance`
  * **Scan:** Makes an adjacent unstable cell permanently stable and traversable for both rovers. The Scout does not move when scanning.
      * **Power Cost:** `3` units.
  * The Scout **cannot** perform the Build or Finalize Site actions.

### Objectives:

1.  **State Space Modeling:** Define a state representation that captures the positions of the Builder and Scout, the dynamic elevation map, and the set of remaining unstable cells.

2.  **Action Generation Logic:** Implement functions to generate all valid actions for each rover type based on its unique movement, abilities, and associated power costs. The logic must enforce all constraints.

3.  **Optimal Path Search:** Utilize a shortest-path algorithm for **weighted graphs** (e.g., Dijkstra's Algorithm) to find the path that minimizes total power consumption. The search must correctly prioritize states with lower accumulated power usage.

4.  **Termination and Impossibility Handling:** The algorithm must terminate when the Builder successfully executes the "Finalize Site" action. If this is impossible, the algorithm must return infinity.

### Constraints:

  * Rovers cannot move onto unstable cells.
  * Rovers cannot occupy the same cell. The Scout cannot move *through* the Builder's cell.
