### Problem Statement

In a cutting-edge research initiative focused on secure multi-party computation (MPC), a global consortium of researchers is developing a cryptographic coordination framework that manages dynamic trust relationships among a large set of participants. The system serves as the backbone of a real-time MPC environment, where multiple entities collaborate securely without revealing their private data. As participants join, leave, or reconfigure their trust links, the framework must dynamically maintain which entities are currently part of the same connected trust group. This ensures that secure computation tasks can continue smoothly, while maintaining strict isolation between untrusted or disconnected parties.

To achieve this, the framework leverages dynamic connectivity structures inspired by the Disjoint-Set (Union-Find) model. It must efficiently process frequent connectivity changes, enforce strong validation rules to prevent invalid operations, and guarantee deterministic outcomes for every connectivity query. The structure should remain stable even under rapid trust updates, invalid merges, or attempts to remove non-existent edges, ensuring robustness and reproducibility in a cryptographic setting.

Supported operations:

* union(u, v) - merge the groups containing u and v and add a direct edge between them.
* split(u, v) - remove the direct edge between u and v. This operation is edge-only and must not affect other edges or paths.
* rekey(u, v) - a logical event that does not modify connectivity.
* find(u, v) - check if u and v are currently in the same connected group.

The system must maintain connectivity efficiently under repeated merges and splits.
