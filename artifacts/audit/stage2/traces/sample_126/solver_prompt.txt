Problem Statement:
### **Problem Statement**

In a modern smart city, municipal agencies are coordinating multiple infrastructure networks such as roads, electricity grids, and rail systems. These networks are modeled as a **multi-layered graph**, where each **layer** represents a distinct infrastructure system.

Each layer consists of **intersections** (nodes) connected by **conduits** (edges). Every conduit has an associated **cost**, representing maintenance, operation, or distance. Some conduits may be **mandatory** (must always remain active due to safety or regulation), while others may be **prohibited** (cannot be used regardless of cost benefit).

The task is to build and dynamically maintain an **Integrated Multi-Layer Spanning Subgraph** — a structure that connects all intersections (across all layers) with the **minimum total cost**, while adapting to changes such as conduit cost updates, new conduit activations, or regulatory mandates.

The algorithm must correctly update the subgraph after every change while maintaining validity (connectivity, minimal cost, and regulatory compliance).
Performance should be computationally efficient and realistic for test cases (for example, within polynomial time).

---

### **Definitions**

* **Layer**: A numbered level of infrastructure (e.g., 0 = roads, 1 = electric grid, 2 = rail network).
* **Intersection (node)**: A unique integer representing a connection point in the infrastructure.
* **Conduit (edge)**: A connection between two intersections in a given layer, represented as `[layer, node1, node2]`.
* **Cost**: A non-negative integer representing the conduit’s weight or expense.
* **Mandatory conduit**: A conduit that must always be included in the spanning subgraph.
* **Prohibited conduit**: A conduit that must not be included in the subgraph under any circumstance.
* **Integrated Multi-Layer Spanning Subgraph**: A minimal-cost set of conduits that ensures all intersections (in all layers) are connected, directly or indirectly, through at least one valid conduit.

---

### **Objectives**

1. **Initial Construction**
   Build the minimum-cost spanning subgraph across all layers that connects all intersections through available conduits.

2. **Regulatory Enforcement**
   Incorporate directives that can:

   * Add **mandatory** conduits (which must be included).
   * Add **prohibited** conduits (which must be excluded).

3. **Dynamic Update Handling**
   Efficiently update the spanning subgraph when:

   * The cost of a conduit changes.
   * A conduit is newly added or decommissioned.
   * A regulatory rule marks a conduit as **MANDATORY** or **PROHIBITED**.

4. **Scalability**
   The algorithm must handle updates efficiently (e.g., logarithmic or near-logarithmic per update is ideal but not strictly required). The focus is on correctness and realistic performance, not hardware-dependent “real-time” constraints.

---

### **Constraints**

* Each conduit is represented as a unique **list** `[layer, node1, node2]`.
* Costs are **non-negative integers** and can change dynamically.
* **Mandatory conduits** cannot be removed.
* **Prohibited conduits** cannot be added to the spanning subgraph.
* All updates are processed sequentially, and after each update, the subgraph must remain connected and valid.
* If two conduits connect the same intersections in different layers, only one of them can be active at a time (to prevent redundant infrastructure overlap).
* When a conduit becomes MANDATORY, any other conduit forming a cycle with it must have higher total cost before being considered for inclusion.
