Problem Statement:
### Problem Statement

A global logistics provider manages a large-scale **delivery network** across multiple regions. Each region is represented as a **city** (graph node), and the **roads** connecting them are **edges** with known distances and varying fuel availability conditions.

Unlike standard shortest-path problems, every city has additional operational constraints:

* Limited fuel availability — refueling is possible only at designated fuel-station cities.
* **Cargo inspection checkpoints** at specific cities that must be visited before proceeding further.
* **Vehicle fuel capacity limits** that restrict how far the vehicle can travel without refueling.

The task is to design an algorithm that finds the **optimal delivery route** from a start city to a destination city while considering:

1. Limited and dynamically available refueling options.
2. Mandatory inspection requirements at specified cities.
3. Fuel consumption per road segment.

The algorithm must find a feasible and minimum-distance route that satisfies all constraints without running out of fuel or skipping required inspections.

---

### Objectives

1. **Find the optimal delivery path** from the start city to the destination city, minimizing total travel distance.
2. Implement a **state-tracking mechanism** that records:

   * Current remaining fuel.
   * Completed or pending cargo inspections.
   * Fuel availability at each city during travel.
3. Allow **adaptive path adjustments**, enabling the algorithm to handle situations where refueling or inspection timing affects route selection.
4. Implement an efficient solution using only **core Python features** (no external libraries).

---

### Constraints

* Each city is represented as a unique integer node.
* Roads are **directed edges** represented as lists `[destination_node, distance]`.
* The vehicle’s **maximum fuel capacity** and **fuel consumption rate** (units per distance) are provided.
* **Fuel stations** are located in specific cities. A vehicle can refuel **only if** that city’s fuel station is open during the current operational time.
* **Inspection checkpoints** are mandatory stops and must be visited before reaching the final destination.
* If no valid path exists under given constraints, the algorithm should return an **empty list `[]`**.
* Graph keys are provided as strings in the input; the algorithm must normalize (convert) them into integer node IDs before processing.
* Normalize city identifiers by converting all graph keys to integers during preprocessing.
---

### Key Definitions

* **City (Node):** A point in the logistics network representing a region.
* **Road (Edge):** A one-way connection between two cities with an associated travel distance.
* **Fuel Capacity (C):** The maximum fuel the vehicle can hold at any point in time.
* **Fuel Consumption Rate (r):** The amount of fuel consumed per unit of distance.

  * Remaining fuel after traveling a road of distance `d` = `current_fuel - (r × d)`
* **Fuel Station:** A city where the vehicle can refuel (partially or fully) depending on the station’s operational availability.
* **Inspection Checkpoint:** A designated city where mandatory cargo inspection must occur before proceeding further.
