Problem Statement:
### **Problem Statement**

A multinational logistics company must efficiently manage the flow of different shipment types across a large network of distribution centers. Each distribution center can dynamically merge or split based on regional demands. The network contains multiple routes (called **lanes**) between centers, and each lane has its own capacity, cost, and priority.

The objective is to develop a **Python algorithm** that computes the most cost-efficient and capacity-balanced shipment flow using a **Min-Cost Max-Flow** strategy that ensures maximum throughput at minimal transportation cost.

The system must also identify **critical connections** (edges) that restrict total flow — known as **bottlenecks** — and adapt dynamically when certain routes or centers become unavailable. This ensures that the logistics system maintains optimal performance even during operational disruptions.

---

### **Objectives**

1. **Graph Representation:**
   Represent the logistics network as a **directed graph** where nodes correspond to distribution centers and edges represent transportation routes (lanes). Each edge should store its capacity, cost, and priority. Multiple parallel edges between the same nodes (multi-lane routes) are allowed.

2. **Flow Optimization (Min-Cost Max-Flow):**
   Implement a **Min-Cost Max-Flow algorithm** to find the maximum possible flow of shipments through the network while minimizing the total cost. The algorithm should support updates when nodes merge or split (representing dynamic reconfiguration of centers).

3. **Dynamic Bottleneck Detection (Min-Cut Analysis):**
   Apply **min-cut principles** to identify which edges most restrict the total feasible flow. These edges are the **critical bottlenecks** that should be prioritized for capacity or route upgrades.

4. **Real-Time Adaptability:**
   Allow the system to **update flow and bottleneck information**  when any route or node becomes temporarily unavailable. Instead of recalculating from scratch, the algorithm should efficiently recompute affected portions of the network.

---

### **Key Definitions**

* **Node:** A distribution center or transfer hub in the logistics network.
* **Edge / Lane:** A directed route from one node to another, with associated `capacity`, `cost`, and `priority`.
* **Capacity:** The maximum quantity of goods that can pass through an edge.
* **Cost:** The expense of transporting one unit of goods along the edge.
* **Min-Cost Max-Flow:** A graph algorithm that finds the highest flow possible between a source and sink while minimizing the total transportation cost.
* **Min-Cut:** A set of edges that, if removed, would prevent any flow from reaching the destination (sink). These represent bottlenecks.
* **Cost:** The transportation expense per unit of goods along an edge, used as the optimization criterion minimized in the Min-Cost Max-Flow computation.
* **Priority:** A weight factor influencing route preference during optimization. Higher priority lanes are favored when costs are similar, acting as a secondary optimization criterion.
