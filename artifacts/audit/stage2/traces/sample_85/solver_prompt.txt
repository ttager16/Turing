### Problem Statement

A global logistics company is developing a **Python-based inventory management system** that uses a **Fenwick Tree (Binary Indexed Tree)** to efficiently manage and query stock levels across multiple warehouses.  
Each warehouse maintains real-time stock data for thousands of product categories. During high-traffic shopping seasons, massive updates and queries occur simultaneously — for example, flash sales, restocks, or inventory corrections.  
The goal is to implement a **dynamic Fenwick Tree** capable of handling:

- **Real-time insertions, deletions, and updates** of stock quantities.  
- **Efficient prefix-sum queries** to report total stock up to a certain index.  
- **Automatic scaling** when the number of tracked categories grows or shrinks.  
- **Overflow prevention and data integrity**, even under extreme transaction volume.

The system must sustain performance and accuracy despite frequent structural mutations, ensuring no data corruption or miscalculated prefix sums.

---

### Objectives

1. **Dynamic Updates:**  
   Implement logic for three operations:
   - `'add'` → Insert a new product quantity at the next available index (expanding the tree if needed).  
   - `'remove'` → Delete a product at a specific index, rebuilding or rebalancing as necessary.  
   - `'update'` → Modify the stock level of an existing product by adding or subtracting a delta.

2. **Efficient Prefix Queries:**  
   Support operations of the form `'query', idx'` that return the cumulative stock level from index `1` to `idx` (inclusive).

3. **Dynamic Resizing:**  
   When `add` or `remove` changes the total number of tracked products, ensure the Fenwick Tree remains consistent without manual reinitialization.

4. **Robust Error Handling:**  
   - Prevent overflows, invalid removals, or negative stock propagation.  
   - If `'remove'` or `'update'` refers to a non-existent index, the operation should be ignored safely.

5. **Performance Constraints:**  
   - Must handle up to **1 million operations** efficiently.  
   - Operations must execute within **O(log n)** average time.  
   - Use **only Python’s standard library**, no external dependencies.
