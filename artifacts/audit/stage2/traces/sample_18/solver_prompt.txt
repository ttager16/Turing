### Problem Statement
In the context of a logistics firm seeking to optimize its multi-layer distribution network, you are tasked with assigning service responsibility for a hierarchy of interconnected regions (organized as an implicit binary tree) to a minimal set of delivery companies. Each region i in the tree has a required demand stored in the array demand[i], and each available company j can handle up to capacities[j] deliveries in a single connected subtree segment.

Unlike a simple one-dimensional or region-by-region allocation, each company’s coverage must form a valid subtree path (from any node down to one of its descendants) such that:
1. No company’s allocated path sum of demands exceeds that company’s capacity.
2. All regions must be covered without overlap gaps (every region’s demand is fulfilled by at least one company covering that region’s node).
3. The tree is implicitly defined by the parent-child relationships (e.g., for region i, its children are 2i+1 and 2i+2, if they exist).

The challenge is that the global distribution structure, combined with each company’s subtree coverage constraint, demands an advanced tree decomposition or dynamic programming on trees, ensuring no greedy or brute-force approach can feasibly handle the worst-case scenario of up to 10^5 regions. Corner cases include regions with zero demand (still requiring formal assignment), regions whose demand alone exceeds any single company’s capacity (forcing multi-company splits), and partial coverage edges that must be combined consistently in ancestor-descendant paths.

Your goal is to develop an optimal assignment strategy that minimizes the total number of companies utilized while strictly adhering to the connectivity and capacity constraints. This involves multi-step reasoning wherein subtrees are subdivided or merged in ways that respect capacity limits, ensuring no region’s demand remains uncovered.

#### Objectives
1. Implement a Python function  
   allocate_minimum_companies(demand: List[int], capacities: List[int]) -> int

- The length of demand is at most 10^5. demand[i] ≥ 0 indicates the number of deliveries required by region i.

- All nodes (regions) in the tree must be assigned coverage by at least one company. Multiple companies can partially overlap on a region if needed, but no region can be left uncovered.  
- A direct or naive assignment that tries all subsets of paths is infeasible given the potential size (up to 10^5 regions). A tree-based dynamic programming or decomposition method should be employed without reliance on external Python libraries.
