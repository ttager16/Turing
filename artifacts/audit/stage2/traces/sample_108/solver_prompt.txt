Problem Statement:
### Problem Statement

You are tasked with creating an objective function to model the cost of a system of thermoelectric plants (`usines`). The goal is to determine the minimum cost to meet a fluctuating energy demand over a series of time steps.

Each plant has a set of operational parameters:

  * A **fixed cost** per kWh.
  * A **minimum (P-min)** production level when it is "on".
  * A **maximum (P-max)** production level, which is not fixed. Instead, it's determined by a **ramping curve**.
  * **Ramping constraints** that define how quickly a plant can increase its production after being turned on. This is modeled as a piecewise linear function where the effective P-max increases the longer the plant has been continuously active.
  * A minimum **"on-time" ($T_{on}$)**: the number of time steps it must remain on after being started.
  * A minimum **"off-time" ($T_{off}$)**: the number of time steps it must remain off after being shut down.

### Objectives

1.  **Minimize Total Cost:** The primary objective is to find the minimum total generation cost across all time steps.
2.  **Meet Demand Exactly:** The total power generated by all "on" plants must exactly match the `demand` for each time step.
3.  **Economic Dispatch:** If multiple plants are "on" simultaneously, the demand must be met by dispatching the cheapest plant (lowest cost per kWh) first, up to its current, ramp-constrained P-max. If more production is needed, the next cheapest plant is used, and so on, until the demand is met.

### Constraints

1.  **Demand Fulfillment:** Total Production = Demand for every time step.
2.  **Production Limits:** If a plant $i$ is on at time $t$, its production $P_{i,t}$ must be $P_{min, i} \le P_{i,t} \le P_{max, i,t}$. If it is off, $P_{i,t} = 0$.
3.  **Ramping Constraints:** The effective maximum production $P_{max, i, t}$ for a plant $i$ at time $t$ depends on how many consecutive time steps it has been "on" up to that point. This is defined by its `ramping_curve`.
4.  **On-Time ($T_{on}$):** If a plant is turned on at time $t$, it cannot be turned off until at least time $t + T_{on}$.
5.  **Off-Time ($T_{off}$):** If a plant is turned off at time $t$, it cannot be turned on until at least time $t + T_{off}$.
6.  **Infeasibility:** Any solution that violates any of the above constraints (e.g., failing to meet demand, violating production limits or $T_{on}$/$T_{off}$) is considered to have an "infinite" cost and is invalid.
